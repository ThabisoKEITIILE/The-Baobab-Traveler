Taste of Botswana

Discover the flavors that tell the story of our land

The Soul of Botswana on Your Plate

Food is more than sustenance in Botswana ‚Äî it's a celebration of community, tradition, and the bounty of our land. Join us as we cook over open fires, share stories around communal pots, and taste dishes that have nourished generations.

Seswaa

The national dish of Botswana, seswaa is slow-cooked, shredded meat that's tender and flavorful. Traditionally prepared for celebrations and communal gatherings, this dish embodies the spirit of togetherness that defines Botswana culture.

Key Ingredients:

Beef or goat meat, salt, water. Slow-cooked until tender and shredded by hand.

Morogo

Wild spinach or morogo is a nutritious leafy green vegetable harvested from the wild. Rich in vitamins and minerals, it's often served with pap and represents our connection to the land and traditional foraging knowledge.

Key Ingredients:

Wild spinach leaves, onions, tomatoes, cooking oil, salt.

Bogobe

A traditional porridge made from sorghum, bogobe is a staple food in Botswana. Served for breakfast or dinner, it provides sustaining energy and connects us to agricultural traditions passed down through generations.

Key Ingredients:

Sorghum meal, water, sometimes fermented for added flavor and nutrition.

Baobab Juice

Made from the fruit of the iconic baobab tree, this tangy, refreshing beverage is packed with vitamin C and antioxidants. It's known as the 'superfruit of Africa' and symbolizes the life-giving nature of the baobab.

Key Ingredients:

Baobab fruit powder, water, honey or sugar to taste.
